# Paper Digest — April 9, 2026

## AI & Machine Learning

### 1. "Claudini: Autoresearch Discovers State-of-the-Art Adversarial Attack Algorithms for LLMs"
*Zhang et al., arXiv:2603.24511, March 2026*

Researchers demonstrate autonomous AI discovery of novel adversarial attacks against large language models using Claude Code as an autoresearch agent. The system iteratively refined existing attack methods like GCG, achieving up to 40% attack success rate on CBRN queries against GPT-OSS-Safeguard-20B (compared to ≤10% for existing methods) and 100% success against Meta-SecAlign-70B. This marks an early proof-of-concept for automated AI research in adversarial robustness, raising important questions about AI-assisted vulnerability discovery while advancing security testing methodologies.

**Significance**: First demonstration that incremental safety and security research can be fully automated using LLM agents.  
**Open Access**: [Available on arXiv](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.24511)

## Health & Longevity

### 2. "From Biological Foundations to Optimizing Performance and Health in Elite Female Winter Olympians"
*Sandbakk et al., Scandinavian Journal of Medicine & Science in Sports, March 2026*

Expert-led narrative review synthesizing sex-based performance differences and training considerations for the Milano Cortina 2026 Winter Olympics (47% female athletes). Research identifies hemoglobin mass, cardiac dimensions, skeletal muscle mass, and body composition as key biological factors underpinning performance gaps. Critically, women exhibit comparable physiological adaptations to men when training is prescribed relative to individual capacity, emphasizing individualized approaches over sex-based generalizations.

**Significance**: Provides evidence-based framework for optimizing female athlete performance and addressing elevated risks of iron deficiency, REDs, and menstrual dysfunction.  
**DOI**: 10.1111/sms.70263

### 3. "Oleuropein Anti-Cancer Effects in Triple-Negative Breast Cancer"
*Kalin et al., Cancer Research, April 2026*

Investigation of oleuropein (olive leaf phenolic compound) effects on MDA-MB-231 breast cancer cells reveals dose-dependent cytotoxicity with 400 µg/mL treatment inducing 3-fold decrease in viable cell density and 8-fold increase in apoptosis. Research demonstrates significant induction of peroxiredoxin antioxidant proteins, particularly Prdx1, suggesting pro-oxidant therapeutic mechanism. Connects Mediterranean diet bioactives to cancer prevention pathways.

**Significance**: Advances understanding of olive bioactives' anti-cancer mechanisms beyond traditional antioxidant properties.  
**DOI**: 10.1158/1538-7445.am2026-3665

## Crypto & Web3

### 4. "Web3 and the Decentralized Future: Data Ownership, Privacy, and Blockchain Infrastructure"
*Krause, SSRN Working Paper, 2026*

Comprehensive analysis of Web3's transformative potential through examination of data ownership models, privacy preservation mechanisms, and Layer-1 blockchain infrastructure foundations. Research highlights the integration challenges and opportunities across big data, artificial intelligence, metaverse, IoT, and privacy computing. Documents significant research acceleration with heightened publication activity in 2022-2023 covering blockchain, decentralized storage, self-sovereign identity, and NFTs.

**Significance**: Establishes framework for understanding Web3's technical foundations and integration pathways with emerging technologies.  
**Available**: SSRN Electronic Library

## Markets & Finance

### 5. "Fintech and Financial Markets: New Research Directions"
*Special Issue, Journal of Financial Economics, March 2026*

Multi-paper examination reveals fintech innovations generate significant benefits including enhanced market access and operational efficiency while introducing new systemic risks through volatility spillovers and interconnectedness. Research includes analysis of client clearing mechanisms showing enhanced netting efficiency for dealers and pricing advantages for clients, plus evaluation of U.S. repo market resilience under institutional stress scenarios exceeding $100 billion in disrupted funding.

**Significance**: Documents the dual nature of fintech innovation as both market enhancer and systemic risk generator, informing regulatory approaches.  
**DOI**: 10.1080/1351847X.2026.2642920
