## Current State: A Perfect Storm of Inflationary Pressures

The Iran conflict triggered what the International Energy Agency characterized as the **"largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market."** Key impacts include:

- **Oil price volatility**: Brent crude surged 10-13% to $80-82/barrel, then fell 15% on ceasefire news but remains 43% above pre-war levels at $96.41
- **Consumer burden**: Americans paid an additional $8.4 billion in fuel expenses within the first month of hostilities
- **Historic fuel shock**: The U.S. experienced the largest fuel cost jump since 1957, with gasoline prices exceeding $4/gallon nationally

### Inflation Trajectory Reversal

Oxford Economics warns that energy price impacts "will push headline CPI inflation well above 3% in March and above 4% by April." This represents a dramatic reversal after early-year cooling, with projections suggesting:

- **March CPI**: 3.3% annually (highest since May 2024)
- **Expected trajectory**: Potentially exceeding 4% by April 2026
- **Cascading effects**: Higher fuel costs rippling through food, transportation, and supply chains

- **Rate cut probability**: CME FedWatch shows only 5.2% chance of rate changes at April 29-30 FOMC meeting
